First Aid for Choking

Steps to Help Someone Who Is Choking

1. Encourage Coughing

Ask the person to keep coughing if they can. Coughing is the most effective way to clear the airway.

2. Perform Back Blows

Stand behind the person, lean them forward slightly, and give up to five firm back blows between the shoulder blades with the heel of your hand.

3. Perform Abdominal Thrusts

Stand behind the person, wrap your arms around their waist, and deliver quick, upward abdominal thrusts until the object is expelled.

4. Alternate Steps

If the airway is still blocked, alternate between five back blows and five abdominal thrusts until help arrives.


5. Call Emergency Services

If the object does not come out or the person becomes unresponsive, call emergency services immediately.

6. Perform CPR if Necessary

If the person becomes unresponsive, begin CPR. Check the mouth for the object between compressions and breaths.

Additional Tips

  • Stay calm and act quickly.
  • Do not perform back blows or abdominal thrusts on someone who is coughing effectively.
  • If the person is pregnant or obese, use chest thrusts instead of abdominal thrusts.
